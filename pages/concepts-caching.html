Simple tip: save stuff that is used more than once in a variable.

  • Don't bother storing anything that is only used once.
  • A common convention is to begin variable names with a $ when it will contain a jQuery object.
  • Sometimes it's helpful to disambiguate "this" by giving it a real name.

Simple Example:

  $('.image').click(function(e){
    e.preventDefault();
    var $clickedImage = $(this);
    ...
  });
