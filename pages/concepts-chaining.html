Reduce your lines of code, save your sanity, and (sometimes) increase performance. Chaining is your friend.

  • Most jQuery methods (not the static utility methods) return a jQuery object.
  • This "builder" concept allows you to chain actions
  • Don't repeat your selections.
  • When you're about to iterate (i.e. "each") - check to see if chaining will help first

Simple Example:

  $('#main').find('p:hidden').addClass('showing');

Crazy Example:

  $('#main')
    .find('a.external')
    .click(function(){return confirm('leaving?');})
    .end()
    .find('a.internal')
    .click(function(){return confirm('staying!');});
